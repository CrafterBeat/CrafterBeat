## � Today's signal · May 2

**🔍 What to Build Today**

The clearest distribution window I see today is the **AI hiring self-preferencing** crack. [AI Self-preferencing in Algorithmic Hiring](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47987256) is burning on the HN front page right now — 314 points, 169 comments — and [@hyperpape](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47987530) dropped this: "I'll copy what I wrote on LinkedIn... they don't actually show that LLMs prefer" (he read half the paper and pointed out the research never actually proves LLMs favor AI-generated content). That sentence alone is a product opportunity. Nobody knows how their resume scores in the eyes of GPT-4o or Claude. Nobody knows whether running their resume through an "AI polish" pass will get them *downranked* by the very algorithmic screeners they're trying to impress. [@danielodievich](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47989204) put it perfectly — 132 upvotes: "loaded qwen/qwen3-v1-30b locally, and fed my 100% human-written resume and asked it 'Make this resume more professional'. Mucho bullets came out." He ran a human resume through Qwen locally and got back a wall of AI-cadence bullet points — exactly the format an ATS might love or hate. The gap: a tool that runs multiple LLM judges simultaneously, compares the original vs. AI-polished version on a scoring diff, and shows candidates exactly what their resume looks like to the algorithm.

## What is CrafterBeat / 这是什么

CrafterBeat is a daily intelligence briefing for indie developers and tech entrepreneurs. Every morning it cross-references 10+ data sources across 22 chapters — from opportunity discovery and tech stack picks to competitive intel and trend signals — and publishes only data-backed findings with specific numbers, links, and actionable takeaways.

CrafterBeat 是给独立开发者和技术创业者的每日情报简报。每天早上交叉验证 10+ 数据源，覆盖 22 个章节（机会发现、技术选型、竞争情报、趋势判断、行动触发），只发布有具体数字、链接和可操作结论的信号。
